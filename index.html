ASCII

This table shows all the characters in the American Standard Code for Information Interchange (ASCII). The column and row headers determine the hexadecimal ASCII code of each character. The column header gives the leading hexadecimal digit, and the row header gives the trailing hexadecimal digit.

The nongraphic ASCII characters are represented by their abbreviations surrounded by dashed boxes. Hover over or tap these characters to see their meanings.

ASCII dates back to the 1960s, and its use in network interchange is described by RFC 20, an official internet standard. ASCII also forms the basis of Unicode, the character encoding used for almost all text on the internet. ASCII is honored as one of the IEEE milestones, achievements that are recognized as being significant in the history of electrical and electronics engineering.
